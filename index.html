Books Genres

A genre is the category or style of a book – for example, romance, crime or horror. It comes from the French word, for 'type. ' In essence, it describes the type of story being told.

Novels

A novel is an invented prose narrative of significant length and complexity that deals imaginatively with human experience. Its roots can be traced back thousands of years, though its origins in English are traditionally placed in the 18th century.

Historical

The main purpose of historical fiction is to provide readers or viewers a chance to see history from a different perspective. Historical fiction offers entertainment value when discussing time periods of history to make events and characters come alive.

Classical

Classical literature refers to the great masterpieces of Greek, Roman, and other ancient civilizations. The works of Homer, Ovid, and Sophocles are all examples of classical literature. It can also include epic, lyric, tragedy, comedy, pastoral, and other forms of writing

IT ENDS WITH US

COLLEN HOVER

It Ends With Us is one of the most powerful books of 2016 and one of the most raw, honest, inspiring, and profoundly beautiful stories I've ever read. The novel tells the story of Lily Bloom and her doomed romance with Ryle Kincaid and traces her past history growing up in an abusive home, her fall into an abusive relationship, and her escape from that relationship.

The Hunchback of Notre-Dame

Victor Hygo

The Hunchback of Notre-Dame is a French Gothic novel by Victor Hugo, published in 1831. It focuses on the unfortunate story of Quasimodo, the Gypsy street dancer Esmeralda and Quasimodo's guardian the Archdeacon Claude Frollo in 15th-century Paris. All its elements —Renaissance setting, impossible love affairs, marginalized characters— make the work a model of the literary themes of Romanticism

The Da Vinci Code

Dan Brown

The Da Vinci Code is a 2003 mystery thriller novel by Dan Brown. It is Brown's second novel to include the character Robert Langdon: the first was his 2000 novel Angels & Demons. The Da Vinci Code follows symbologist Robert Langdon and cryptologist Sophie Neveu after a murder in the Louvre Museum in Paris causes them to become involved in a battle between the Priory of Sion and Opus Dei over the possibility of Jesus Christ and Mary Magdalene having had a child together.

Libraries in Kosovo

Dukagjini & Buzuku

Dukagjini Bookshop On 4 May 2000, in Kosovo’s capital, Pristina, was added a new place for books. This is the first International and National Bookshop “Dukagjini”, opened after the war. This Bookshop, now have more than 25.000+ titles, in different languages: Albanian, English, France, Spanish, Italian and a lot of other european and world languages. What makes this bookshop, even more frequented, is the large number of publications, issued by the publishing house “Dukagjini”
"You can find Buzuku Bookstore in both City center of Prishtina (in Edit Durham sq., as well as Mother Teresa sq., in front of Ministry of Culture as well as mall Albi Mall (3rd floor). Very caring and welcoming personnel that will help finding the book you'll like to read, be it in the Albanian language or English language. Best price in both languages.
